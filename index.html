  • 01 What do you think is the root cause of the struggles that those with mental illness and substance abuse face?

    It's not easy for anyone to have a job. But for those who struggle with addictions or mental illnesses, it can be especially hard to hold down a steady job. Even if they manage to find and keep employment, people with mental illness and substance abuse problems often still have trouble being productive at work.

    Many people might assume that these problems are caused by some sort of laziness: If a person has the energy to use drugs or alcohol, then why don't they have the energy to get up early and go to work? And isn't it their own fault for not being motivated enough to stop using?

    But people with these types of problems are often battling much bigger obstacles, such as brain chemistry, genetic factors, or environmental factors that may make it difficult or impossible for them to control their actions. In fact, just being born into a lower-class neighborhood can increase your likelihood of developing drug or alcohol addiction by 40%. The point here is that these issues are much more complicated than they may appear on the surface—and it's important to understand this complexity when trying to solve them.

  • 02 How do you feel about the future of technology?

    This is not a simple question to answer. There are so many factors involved. If you're like us, you feel that the future of technology is scary at times—but also thrilling, with endless potential for change.

    It's undeniable that there will be some challenges as we go forward, but we believe that we have the ability to overcome them if we work together.

  • 03 What would you like to see happen in the world of work and technology?

    We think we could use a lot more people who are willing to go out on a limb and take risks. We'd love to see more people be passionate about their ideas and follow through with them, no matter how crazy it sounds.

    We think if people can learn to rely on themselves and follow their dreams, they'll be happier in the long run, and they'll also make the world a better place.

    We'd also like to see more jobs that focus on helping others. It makes us sad that there are so many people out there who are struggling, when the resources are there to help them.
